# DiffusionLimitedReaction

**Source:** `models/chemical_reactions/DiffusionLimitedReaction.py`

Diffusion-limited reaction rate for a shrinking-core product phase.

Given the dimensionless inner radius $r_i$ and outer radius
$r_o$ of the product phase, the liquid and solid reactivities
$R_l$ and $R_s$ (both in $[0, 1]$), the characteristic
diffusion coefficient $D$ of the rate-limiting species, and the
molar volume $\omega$ of that species, the volumetric reaction
rate is

$$
\dot{\alpha} = \frac{2 D R_l R_s}{\omega}
               \cdot \frac{r_o}{r_o - r_i + \delta}
$$

where $\delta$ is a small "dummy thickness" added to the product
thickness $r_o - r_i$ to keep the rate finite at the start of the
reaction.

## Inputs

`product_inner_radius` &mdash; `input` &middot; `Scalar` &middot; **required**
: Inner radius of the product phase

`solid_inner_radius` &mdash; `input` &middot; `Scalar` &middot; **required**
: Inner radius of the solid phase

`liquid_reactivity` &mdash; `input` &middot; `Scalar` &middot; **required**
: Reactivity of the liquid phase, between 0 and 1

`solid_reactivity` &mdash; `input` &middot; `Scalar` &middot; **required**
: Reactivity of the solid phase, between 0 and 1


## Outputs

`reaction_rate` &mdash; `output` &middot; `Scalar` &middot; **required**
: Product phase substance volumetric rate of change


## Parameters

`diffusion_coefficient` &mdash; `parameter` &middot; `Scalar` &middot; **required**
: Diffusion coefficient of the rate-limiting species in the product phase


## Other options

`product_dummy_thickness` &mdash; `float` &middot; default `0.01`
: Minimum product thickness to avoid division by 0

`molar_volume` &mdash; `float` &middot; **required**
: Molar volume of the rate-limiting (liquid) species
