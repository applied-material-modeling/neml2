# CylindricalChannelGeometry

**Source:** `models/chemical_reactions/CylindricalChannelGeometry.py`

Dimensionless inner/outer radii of a cylindrical reaction product.

Given the volume fractions $\phi_s$ (solid) and $\phi_p$
(product), define $\mathrm{cap} = 1 - \phi_s - \phi_p$. Then

$$
r_i = \sqrt{\operatorname{clamp}(\mathrm{cap}, \varepsilon, 1 - \varepsilon)},
\qquad
r_o = \sqrt{1 - \phi_s}.
$$

The clamp guards `sqrt` against negative arguments and the flat
saturation tails kill the derivative of $r_i$ exactly as in the
C++ source.

## Inputs

`solid_fraction` &mdash; `input` &middot; `Scalar` &middot; **required**
: Volume fraction of the solid phase

`product_fraction` &mdash; `input` &middot; `Scalar` &middot; **required**
: Volume fraction of the product phase


## Outputs

`inner_radius` &mdash; `output` &middot; `Scalar` &middot; **required**
: Dimensionless inner radius of the product phase

`outer_radius` &mdash; `output` &middot; `Scalar` &middot; **required**
: Dimensionless outer radius of the product phase
